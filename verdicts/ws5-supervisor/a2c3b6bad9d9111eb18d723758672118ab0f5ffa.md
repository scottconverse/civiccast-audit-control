## Executive result

`CHANGES_REQUIRED` is returned for exactly `a2c3b6bad9d9111eb18d723758672118ab0f5ffa`. The committed suites are broad and pass, but fresh runtime falsifications found three Critical and five Major functional defects in the supervision paths those suites do not cover.

Most importantly, the supervisor starts a normal control plane before it observes a held maintenance interlock, does not run the continuous dual-runtime guard during its main loop, and tracks the short-lived `pg_ctl start` launcher rather than the PostgreSQL server. These are direct failures of the maintenance dual-transmission rule, exclusive-runtime enforcement, and durable child containment. RAT-001 and RAT-004 therefore remain open. RAT-002's composite exit event and RAT-003's named-pipe mask survive the scoped falsifications, but they cannot compensate for the broken surrounding runtime.

This verdict does not authorize integration, merge, tag, release, installation, service registration, selector mutation, or cutover. Any corrective source change produces a new SHA and requires a new audit.

## Findings

### CC-WS5-001 - Critical - Maintenance entry fails open with a normal control plane

`Supervisor.run()` calls `start()` before the first interlock poll at `civiccast/native/supervisor/core.py:573` and `civiccast/native/supervisor/core.py:582`. `start()` launches the control plane in normal mode. When the first poll then reports a held interlock, `_reconcile_maintenance()` leaves an already-ready control plane running instead of replacing it with an attested maintenance instance at `civiccast/native/supervisor/core.py:606`.

The auditor executed both boot-time and ready-to-maintenance transitions with instrumented process and health seams. A held interlock at boot reached state `maintenance` with exactly one control-plane spawn whose environment contained mode `normal`. A ready supervisor whose interlock changed to held retained the same normal control-plane PID while reporting `maintenance`. A normal-mode control plane can therefore retain worker and mutating behavior during the freeze, violating RAT-001's enforceable cross-process contract and the design's dual-transmission requirement.

The same path also creates an unbounded duplicate-spawn failure: if the maintenance child remains unattested, each held-state tick launches another control plane without terminating or rejecting the live prior handle. Two ticks after maintenance entry produced three simultaneously live control-plane handles and no termination.

**Blast radius:** every maintenance entry, including an owner-directed freeze and a guard-triggered freeze, can leave write-capable work active or multiply live control planes while the supervisor reports the safe maintenance state.

**Required acceptance:** poll and enforce the interlock before any writer-capable child starts; on maintenance entry, prove a controlled transition from the normal child to one and only one contract-versioned, fully attested maintenance child; refuse readiness without the full attestation; and add executable boot-held, ready-to-held, unattested-live, and repeated-tick controls that assert both state and process identity.

### CC-WS5-002 - Critical - The continuous dual-runtime guard is never run

The production supervisor uses the guard only through a pre-child `evaluate_once()` call at `civiccast/native/supervisor/core.py:511`. Its `run()` and `tick()` paths do not call or schedule `GuardMonitor.run()`, even though the monitor loop exists at `civiccast/native/runtime_guard.py:475`. The configured `guard_interval_seconds` at `civiccast/native/supervisor/config.py:88` is consequently unused by the supervisor service.

The auditor started the supervisor, allowed it to become ready, changed the guard seam from `start` to a WSL-active/refuse decision, and executed three ticks. State remained `ready`, the control plane remained alive, `evaluate_once()` was called zero times after startup, and only the single pre-child call had occurred. A selector flip or WSL activation during operation is therefore not detected and cannot trigger the required controlled stop.

**Blast radius:** simultaneous native and WSL writers can arise after startup, defeating charter item 4's runtime-exclusivity invariant and the WS5 requirement to integrate that guard continuously.

**Required acceptance:** integrate the guard monitor into the service lifecycle at the configured interval; route a non-start decision through the ratified supervisor transition and controlled-stop path; join/stop the monitor cleanly; and prove a mid-operation selector flip and WSL activation both stop or freeze native writers without waiting for a child restart.

### CC-WS5-003 - Critical - PostgreSQL ownership tracks `pg_ctl`, not the database server

The PostgreSQL child command is `pg_ctl start -D ... -w` at `civiccast/native/supervisor/children.py:224`. The runner returns that command's process handle, and the supervisor assigns that PID to the Job Object. `pg_ctl start` exits after the postmaster reports ready; it is a launcher, not the long-running PostgreSQL server. The supervisor therefore monitors and contains the wrong process.

Using a realistic launcher seam that exited immediately while the PostgreSQL readiness probe succeeded, initial startup reported success. Five ticks then produced six PostgreSQL launches, a restart count of five, and state `degraded`. The actual postmaster was not represented by the tracked handle. The graceful-stop path can likewise accept a dead launcher while `pg_ctl stop` is invoked with `check=False`, leaving no proof that the postmaster stopped or belongs to the Job Object.

**Blast radius:** restart storms can create or contend with multiple postmasters; supervisor death is not proven to reap PostgreSQL; shutdown can report completion without a WAL-safe database stop; AC2, AC3, and AC4 claims for the real PostgreSQL child are invalidated.

**Required acceptance:** acquire and validate the real postmaster PID/handle after `pg_ctl start`, assign that durable process to the Job Object before declaring readiness, monitor that handle, make failed `pg_ctl stop -m fast` observable and escalatable, and prove real PostgreSQL start, unexpected death, restart, service stop, and supervisor-death containment on Windows.

### CC-WS5-004 - Major - RAT-004's worker drain starts with forced termination

`EgressDaemon.stop_all_channels()` calls `_stop()` at `civiccast/egress/daemon.py:1114`; `_stop()` immediately calls `_process_terminate(process)` at `civiccast/egress/daemon.py:1112`. The post-deadline branch calls the same termination primitive again at line 1167. No production caller sends a terminal `stop` command through the D2 worker channel before that first terminate. The committed multi-channel and hung-worker tests encode the first terminate as the supposed graceful action rather than falsifying it.

This is the exact behavior RAT-004 held: there is no initial protocol-level terminal request followed by observed process exit and deadline-only escalation. Calling the method from the control-plane lifespan does not alter the method's forced-termination semantics.

**Blast radius:** active GStreamer workers can be terminated before they receive their terminal protocol action, losing the promised graceful drain behavior and making the Job Object/deadline separation untrue.

**Required acceptance:** send the versioned terminal worker command first, observe OS process exit as ground truth, reserve forced termination for processes still alive after the deadline, and add tests that fail if terminate occurs before expiry, including lost-ack-but-exited and hung-worker cases.

### CC-WS5-005 - Major - Recovery wedges live-unready children and omits dependent restart/backoff

`_recover_dead_children()` acts only when a handle is absent or no longer alive at `civiccast/native/supervisor/core.py:639`. A live control plane that misses its readiness deadline is marked failed but is not stopped, retried, or later promoted when health becomes good. The auditor reproduced that path: after timeout the state remained `starting`; changing health to ready and ticking caused zero new spawns and no state transition.

The auditor also killed PostgreSQL after a ready start. Only PostgreSQL was relaunched; the existing NATS and control-plane PIDs were retained, contrary to D6's controlled dependent restart. `backoff_with_jitter()` is defined at `civiccast/native/supervisor/children.py:518` but has no production caller, so retries occur once per tick rather than with the specified exponential jittered backoff. The `recovered` state event is likewise not dispatched by production recovery.

**Blast radius:** transient readiness failures can wedge the service indefinitely; database replacement can leave dependents bound to invalid state; real failures can become tight restart loops and misleading degraded/ready status.

**Required acceptance:** explicitly terminate and retry live-unready children; implement per-child scheduled jittered backoff and stable restart accounting; restart downstream dependents in the declared order after a database death; dispatch recovery only after fresh readiness; and add clock-controlled runtime tests for each case, including the claimed retry-until-ready F-REV-3 scenario.

### CC-WS5-006 - Major - The D2 worker channel loses immediate acknowledgements and has no production reconnect lifecycle

`_WindowsPipeChannel.send_and_wait()` sends through the session before installing its waiter at `civiccast/egress/gst/strategy.py:359`. With an immediate-ack transport, the auditor observed the acknowledgement being applied before correlation existed; `send_and_wait()` returned `False`. On timeout, the result of `session.expire()` is ignored. `WorkerPipeSession.reconnect()` exists at line 172 but has no production caller, and `GstPlayoutStrategy.close_channel()` at line 557 has no caller, leaving old pipe servers/lifecycles unclosed on replacement.

**Blast radius:** fast local worker replies can be reported as failures, commands are not reliably replayed after reconnect, and same-channel restart can leak or conflict with the old named-pipe server. The pure replay-ledger tests do not prove the wired production seam.

**Required acceptance:** register correlation before write, make timeout/lost-ack outcomes explicit, wire reconnect replay and channel closure into worker replacement and application shutdown, and prove immediate ack, disconnect-before-ack, reconnect replay, duplicate replay, and same-channel replacement through the production strategy.

### CC-WS5-007 - Major - The service/control composition is not a usable production supervisor

`build_control_pipe()` binds directly to `supervisor.command_handler()` at `civiccast/native/supervisor/service.py:604`, but that handler implements only `status` and `version` at `civiccast/native/supervisor/core.py:731`. The auditor invoked the authorized admin verbs `start`, `stop`, `restart`, `drain`, and `runtime_set`; every call raised `ValueError` stating that mutating commands are wired at the service layer, yet no service-layer wrapper supplies them.

`build_service_class()` requires an injected service factory and `build_production_service()` itself requires injected guard, outbox, database, NATS, and health seams. No production entry point composes these functions. Pipe-open/squat failure is logged but is not converted into the specified degraded state, Event Log signal, and retry/backoff lifecycle. No shipped client verifies the SYSTEM server identity before sending commands.

**Blast radius:** the advertised Windows service cannot be installed/run from a complete in-repo composition, privileged control verbs are unavailable, and pipe failure/identity requirements are not enforced end to end.

**Required acceptance:** provide the installable service entry point and concrete production seam composition; implement and authorize every declared command with idempotent semantics; bind pipe failure to supervisor degradation plus retry and Event Log; ship the server-identity-verifying client path; and execute the service/control acceptance panel in Session 0 and non-admin/admin clients.

### CC-WS5-009 - Minor - Literal unknown supervisor mode normalizes to normal

`_supervisor_mode()` at `civiccast/app.py:470` treats every value other than literal `maintenance` as `normal`. The auditor set `CIVICCAST_SUPERVISOR_MODE=unknown` with contract version `1`; the function returned `normal`. This contradicts the handoff's claim that unknown mode fails closed. The maintenance readiness verifier rejects a normal attestation, but a directly or incorrectly launched control plane starts normal writer-capable behavior rather than refusing startup.

**Required acceptance:** reject an explicitly present unknown mode (or enter a documented non-writer fail-closed mode) and cover missing-for-backward-compatibility separately from invalid-present input.
