## Findings

### CC-WS3-008 - Major - Governed Postgres restore claim resolves through a capture-only marker and entry

- **Dimension:** claim-to-evidence association / merge resolution / functional false pass
- **Contract:** v13 D9 at `.agent-runs/native-windows/specs/spec-claims-evidence-rule.md:246-250` includes the WS2 drill and every strong token currently in `civiccast/dr/__init__.py`. D1 requires strong-token prose in a governed document to reference its registry entry; D2 then binds that entry's claim-defining code and executable test.
- **Final-tree evidence:** `civiccast/dr/__init__.py:22` asserts “Postgres backup/restore is implemented AND executed.” The bullet's only relevant marker at line 52 is `# claim:ws2-postgres-backup-capture`.
- **Registry evidence:** `docs/claims/claims.yaml:104-112` defines `ws2-postgres-backup-capture`, states that its bound test exercises capture only, and explicitly defers the recovery/restore claim to follow-up. Its test role is `tests/dr/test_postgres_backup.py::test_postgres_backup_snapshot_matches_source`, not the restore drill.
- **Executed falsification:** against the exact merged tree, `scan_marker_violations(...)` returned `[]`, the full registry reported zero marker/blob/capability violations, and live CI printed `claims-evidence: PASS`. Printing the referenced entry confirmed its capture-only claim text while the governed block asserted restore.
- **Why CI does not cure it:** exact-head CI separately executed 20/20 Postgres restore-drill tests with zero skips. That proves the inherited WS2 implementation ran; it does not bind the governed restore sentence to those code/test identities through the claims registry. Future restore drift would not invalidate the capture-only entry.
- **Consequence:** a marker for one registered capability can satisfy D1 for a broader capability in the same paragraph, allowing a governed claim outside the referenced entry's proof scope to pass. This is the same class of claim/evidence mismatch the gate exists to prevent, now introduced by the required base merge.
- **Smallest acceptance criterion:** register the Postgres restore claim at the current head as its own same-run entry, bind all restore claim-defining modules and the real end-to-end shipping-dispatch test, and place its distinct marker adjacent to the restore assertion. Add an expected-red regression showing that the current backup/restore block with only the capture marker is rejected; the scanner must not allow a capture-only entry/test to satisfy the block's `restore` capability. The existing capture entry may remain separate.
- **Blast radius:** D1/D2 integrity for governed multi-capability paragraphs; the motivating WS2 restore claim; future drift invalidation after WS2 entered branch ancestry.
